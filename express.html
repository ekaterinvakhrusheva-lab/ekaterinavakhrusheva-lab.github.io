eXpress

I redesigned key flows in a large-scale enterprise communication platform (2M+ users) to make navigation clearer, reduce cognitive load, and help users reach messages, calls, and collaboration tools faster.

Scope

UX research, UX/UI design, prototyping, design system

My role

Product Designer (end-to-end)

Platform

Web, mobile

Timeline

2024 – present

Context

The product had grown for years: new features were shipped, but information architecture and key entry points didn’t evolve at the same pace. As a result, users struggled to locate important actions, and teams had to compensate with onboarding and support.

The challenge

  • Fragmented navigation and inconsistent patterns across modules
  • High cognitive load for daily workflows (messages, calls, files)

Research stages

I started by mapping where users get stuck, then validated hypotheses through quick iterations and usability testing.

1) UX audit + journey mapping

Reviewed key flows end-to-end, identified friction points, and aligned findings with product/engineering on priorities.

  • Heuristic evaluation for navigation & findability
  • IA map: entry points → key actions → dead ends
  • [Optional] support tickets / feedback clustering

2) Iterative prototyping

Built quick prototypes, tested variations of navigation labels, grouping, and entry points, and iterated weekly.

  • Low-to-mid fidelity prototypes in Figma
  • Explored 2–3 IA options and validated with stakeholders
  • Prepared handoff-ready specs for engineering

3) Usability testing

Confirmed that the redesigned IA reduces search time and improves confidence in completing key tasks.

  • Task success, time-on-task, and error rate tracking
  • Validated copy & iconography comprehension
  • [Add] sample size + user segments

Solution

The solution focused on making the product easier to scan and navigate, while keeping it scalable for future modules.

Navigation efficiency & information hierarchy

I introduced clearer grouping, surfaced key actions, and reduced ambiguity in labels, so users could locate features faster.

Before — fragmented entry points and inconsistent patterns
After — clearer hierarchy and quicker access to daily actions

Personalized UI & feature onboarding

Added lightweight personalization and short onboarding flows so new features become discoverable without overwhelming users.

Consistency & scalability (design system)

Built reusable patterns and components to unify the experience across modules and speed up delivery.
