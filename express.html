Overview

I led the redesign of an enterprise communication platform with messaging, calls, and collaboration tools. After 8+ years without significant updates, the product accumulated outdated UI patterns and increasingly complex navigation.

My focus was not just visual refresh, but structural clarity — simplifying core workflows, improving accessibility, and building a scalable design system to support faster delivery across teams.

Challenge

eXpress hadn’t been redesigned in over 8 years. While the product was functionally robust, the experience became harder to navigate over time — patterns diverged across modules, key actions were buried, and everyday flows took too many steps.

The result was increased cognitive load: users spent time searching instead of communicating, and new features were harder to discover and adopt.

Research

To understand what was slowing users down, I combined an audit of key journeys with scaled feedback (survey + interviews), then iterated solutions through prototyping and usability testing.

Discovery

I mixed a UX audit (to see where people lose time) with broader feedback (to confirm what hurts the most), then iterated prototypes with product + engineering.

UX audit + workflow mapping

Mapped key tasks and pinpointed “dead ends”, duplicated patterns, and missing entry points.

Survey (411 responses)

Validated pain points at scale and aligned stakeholders on what to fix first.

Iterative prototyping → delivery

Explored IA options, converged on the simplest scalable model, supported implementation + QA.

What I looked at in the audit
  • Findability: can users predict where a feature lives?
  • Consistency: do similar actions behave the same across the product?
  • Effort: how many steps does a daily task really take?

What we changed

1) Navigation that matches how people work

We simplified the structure so the most common actions are one obvious step away. Less hunting, fewer “where was that again?” moments.

  • Clearer hierarchy + fewer competing entry points
  • Labels and placements became predictable across sections
  • Designed to scale as more features ship

2) Before / After: everyday flows

Instead of redesigning “everything”, we targeted the parts people touch daily: chats, calls, and switching contexts.

3) Design system to stop UI drift

We built shared components and guidelines so the product stays consistent as teams ship. This also made handoff smoother and delivery faster.

  • Reusable components + tokens
  • Patterns for navigation, lists, actions, empty states
  • QA support to keep implementation aligned

Impact

After rollout, we tracked usability outcomes and sentiment shifts. The biggest win was making daily actions faster and more predictable.
